The Science of Data

Data science is a broad interdisciplinary domain aiming at extracting strategic knowledge and relevant insights from datasets of varied nature and sizes, making use of background skills and advanced tools from diverse areas such as computer science and coding, applied mathematics, statistics and data visualization.

As virtually all segments of society generate and store astounding amounts of information, data science has found an unlimited universe of potential applications and implementation scenarios, including business, finance, science, healthcare and education.

Recognized as a foundation pillar of artificial intelligence, data science has gained significant traction and relevance over the past decade, particularly due to the ascent of machine learning.

Predicting with Intelligent Learning Machines

The strategic importance of extracting useful insights from data is obvious. Predicting the future from such intuition and providing individuals and organizations with the relevant elements for decision making is the ultimate, supreme goal in this program. As the amount of data to be accessed, organized, processed and interpreted grows exponentially, computer science has been a central piece of any data science initiative.

Computing and coding have enabled most of the progress experienced by mankind in the past seven decades. Until recently, the traditional, unidirectional approach to computation was purely based on selecting input data and developing code to process it, so that useful output data is obtained. A computing machine could then be reduced to a simple 'processing' entity, transforming inputs into outputs without any self-developed intelligence. However, in several scenarios the inputs and outputs were known, and a new sort of computing logic - where machines should be able to 'learn' and develop its own processing rules - was demanded.

Machine learning is a field of computer science that aims at providing computing engines with the ability to learn and proceed without being explicitly programmed to do so. In such new paradigm, machine learning is an approach to data analysis that comprises building and adapting models that may 'learn' through experience. In other words, machine learning is about constructing algorithmic models that adapt themselves to improve their ability to make predictions through learning.

Primary Goals

This personal website includes selected case studies developed by the author to spread knowledge, raise interest and illustrate how data science & machine learning can assist in addressing or solving a variety of demands identified in several segments, as well as to highlight the author's personal problem-solving and coding skills.

An educational approach is pivotal in this work. Code in Python or R is supplied in its entirety, always accompanied by detailed explanations of the posed challenge, the algorithmic logic utilized to address it and code development guidance. Although coding may not be understood or dominated by most page visitors, both content and language are intended to be as accessible and simple as possible to facilitate comprehension and knowledge dissemination.

Visitors are very welcome and encouraged to benefit from the insights and to fork code pieces included herein at their own discretion. The author strives and dedicates significant amounts of time and energy to develop great content and display it with elegance and accuracy. Please give credit when publishing any work based on this material and include appropriate citations and references.
